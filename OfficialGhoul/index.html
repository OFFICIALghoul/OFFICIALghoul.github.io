What is Ghoul?

Ghoul is a simple encryption/decryption tool that uses the Caesar Cipher method. It is a simple substitution cipher that replaces a letter with the letter a certain number of positions down the alphabet.

For example, with a shift of 1, A would be replaced by B, B would become C, and so on.

It is named after Julius Caesar, who used it in his private correspondence.

Encryption is the process of encoding a message or information in such a way that only authorized parties can access it and those who are not authorized cannot.

Decryption is the process of taking encoded or encrypted text or other data and converting it back into text that you or the computer can read and understand.

How to use Ghoul?

First, you need to choose whether you want to encrypt or decrypt a message.

Then, you need to insert the message you want to encrypt/decrypt.

After that, you need to insert a number between 1-20, which will be used to encrypt/decrypt the message.

Finally, you need to click the button and the result will be displayed.

For example, if you want to encrypt the message "Hello" with a shift of 3, the result will be "Khoor".

And if you want to decrypt the message "Khoor" with a shift of 3, the result will be "Hello".
